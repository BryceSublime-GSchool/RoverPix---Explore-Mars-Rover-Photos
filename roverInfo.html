Curiosity


Primary mission duration: 668 sols ( 687 Earth days )

Current mission duration:



Mission Goals




  • Investigating martian climate and geology, assessment of whether or not the Gale Crater has ever offered environmental conditions favorable for microbial life (water and planetary habitability).

  • Investigate the building blocks of life (carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, oxygen, phosphorus, and sulfur).

  • Identify features that may represent the effects of biological processes (biosignatures and biomolecules).

Cameras



  • Mast Camera - MastCam - True Color images 1600X1200px, 720p video. 8 gigabytes of memory.

  • Chemistry and Camera complex - ChemCam - two different instruments: laser-induced breakdown spectroscopy and a Remote Micro Imager. The laser vaporizes soil samples up to 23 feet away from the drone and the Remote Micro Imager provides a high-resolution image of the sample.

  • NavCam - Two pairs of black and white cameras mounted on the mast and create a stereoscopic 3-D image.

  • Hazard Avoidance Camera - HazCams - four pairs of black and white cameras. They are used for autonomous hazard avoidance during rover drives and for safe positioning of the robotic arm on rocks and soils.

  • Mars Hand Lens Imager - MAHLI - MAHLI is a camera on the rover's robotic arm, and acquires microscopic images of rock and soil.

  • Mars Descent Imager - MARDI - During the descent to the Martian surface, MARDI took color images at 1600×1200 pixels with a 1.3-millisecond exposure time starting at distances of about 3.7 km (2.3 mi) to near 5 m (16 ft) from the ground, at a rate of four frames per second for about two minutes.
